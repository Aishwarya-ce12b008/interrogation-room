## TIER 1: FELONIES (Heavy)

### ROBBERY

**Definition:** Taking property from another person by force or threat of force.

**Variants:**
- Armed Robbery (with weapon)
- Strong-arm Robbery (physical force, no weapon)
- Carjacking
- Home Invasion Robbery
- Bank Robbery

**Key Questions to Ask:**
- Was anyone hurt?
- Was a weapon involved? What kind?
- How much was taken?
- Was this planned or spontaneous?
- Were there accomplices?
- Where is the stolen property now?

**Legal Consequences (US General):**
| Severity | Prison Time | Fines |
|----------|------------|-------|
| Simple Robbery | 2-5 years | Up to $10,000 |
| Armed Robbery | 5-15 years | Up to $25,000 |
| Aggravated (injury) | 10-25 years | Up to $50,000 |
| With Prior Record | 15-life | Varies |

**Mitigating Factors:**
- No weapon used
- No physical harm
- First offense
- Returned stolen property
- Cooperated fully
- Acting under duress

**Aggravating Factors:**
- Weapon used
- Victim injured
- Victim was elderly/child
- Prior criminal record
- Large amount taken
- Gang involvement

**Typical Perpetrator Profile:**
- Desperate financial situation
- Substance abuse issues
- Prior involvement in crime
- Impulsive decision-making

**Victim Impact Statement Themes:**
- "I don't feel safe anymore"
- "I can't sleep at night"
- "I had to move/change jobs"
- "I lost more than money—I lost my sense of security"

### EMBEZZLEMENT

**Definition:** Theft of assets by a person in a position of trust or responsibility.

**Variants:**
- Employee theft
- Executive fraud
- Payroll fraud
- Billing schemes
- Expense reimbursement fraud
- Check tampering

**Key Questions to Ask:**
- How long has this been going on?
- What's the total amount?
- How did you do it without getting caught?
- Did anyone else know?
- Where did the money go?
- Why did you start?
- Are there records/documentation?

**Legal Consequences (US General):**
| Amount | Classification | Prison Time |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| Under $1,000 | Misdemeanor | Up to 1 year |
| $1,000-$20,000 | Felony | 1-5 years |
| $20,000-$100,000 | Felony | 3-10 years |
| Over $100,000 | Grand Theft | 5-20 years |
| Over $1 million | Federal charges | 10-30 years |

**Additional Consequences:**
- Restitution (pay back everything)
- Forfeiture of retirement/benefits
- Professional license revocation
- Lifetime ban from certain industries
- Civil lawsuits from employer
- Tax fraud charges if unreported

**Mitigating Factors:**
- Financial hardship (medical bills, family emergency)
- First offense
- Full cooperation and disclosure
- Willingness to make restitution
- Substance abuse/gambling addiction (shows path to treatment)
- Self-reported before discovery

**Aggravating Factors:**
- Premeditated scheme
- Long duration
- Sophisticated methods
- Betrayal of vulnerable people (elderly, nonprofits)
- Used position of authority
- No remorse

**Typical Perpetrator Profile:**
- Trusted, long-term employee
- Financial pressure (debt, lifestyle, addiction)
- Rationalization ("I deserve this," "I'll pay it back")
- Often no prior criminal history

**Red Flags Detectives Mention:**
- "The audit found discrepancies"
- "Your co-workers noticed lifestyle changes"
- "The numbers don't add up"
- "We've been watching the accounts"

### HIT AND RUN

**Definition:** Leaving the scene of an accident without stopping to identify yourself or render aid.

**Variants:**
- Property damage only
- Injury to another person
- Fatality (vehicular manslaughter)
- Pedestrian/cyclist victim
- Multiple victims

**Key Questions to Ask:**
- Did you know you hit someone/something?
- Did you see the person after impact?
- Why didn't you stop?
- Did you call 911?
- Where is the vehicle now?
- Have you been drinking or using substances?
- Did anyone see you?

**Legal Consequences (US General):**
| Severity | Classification | Prison Time | License |
|----------|---------------|-------------|---------|
| Property only | Misdemeanor | 6 months-1 year | 6 month suspension |
| Injury | Felony | 1-5 years | 1-3 year revocation |
| Serious injury | Felony | 2-10 years | Permanent revocation |
| Fatality | Felony | 4-15 years | Permanent revocation |
| Fatality + DUI | Felony | 10-25 years | Permanent revocation |

**Additional Consequences:**
- Civil wrongful death/injury lawsuit
- Points on driving record
- Increased insurance (if allowed to drive again)
- Potential deportation for non-citizens
- Employment restrictions (CDL, delivery jobs)

**Mitigating Factors:**
- Panicked (didn't think clearly)
- Returned to scene later
- Called anonymously to report
- Victim injuries were minor
- First offense
- Turned self in

**Aggravating Factors:**
- Victim died
- Driver was intoxicated
- Fled at high speed
- Victim was child/elderly
- Attempted to cover up evidence
- Multiple victims

**Psychological Profile:**
- Fight-or-flight response (panic)
- Fear of consequences overwhelming moral response
- "Maybe they're okay" rationalization
- Often haunted by decision afterward

**The Question That Haunts:**
- "Did you look back?"
- "In that moment, did you think about whether they were alive?"
- "What stopped you from calling 911?"

### ASSAULT

**Definition:** Intentionally causing physical harm to another person.

**Variants:**
- Simple Assault (minor injury)
- Aggravated Assault (serious injury or weapon)
- Assault with Deadly Weapon
- Domestic Assault
- Sexual Assault
- Assault on Public Official

**Key Questions to Ask:**
- What happened that led to this?
- Did you throw the first punch?
- What did you use? (hands, object, weapon)
- How badly was the victim hurt?
- What's your relationship to the victim?
- Was anyone else involved?
- Have you done this before?

**Legal Consequences (US General):**
| Type | Classification | Prison Time |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| Simple Assault | Misdemeanor | Up to 1 year |
| Assault w/ Injury | Felony | 1-5 years |
| Aggravated Assault | Felony | 5-20 years |
| Assault w/ Deadly Weapon | Felony | 5-15 years |
| Domestic Assault | Varies | 1-10 years |

**Additional Consequences:**
- Restraining order
- Anger management classes (mandatory)
- Loss of gun rights
- Custody implications (domestic)
- Civil lawsuit for damages
- Permanent violent offender record

**Mitigating Factors:**
- Self-defense (partial)
- Provocation
- Protecting another person
- Heat of passion (not premeditated)
- First offense
- Victim not seriously injured
- Genuine remorse

**Aggravating Factors:**
- Weapon used
- Victim severely injured
- Victim was vulnerable (child, elderly, disabled)
- Premeditated
- Hate crime motivation
- Pattern of violence

### BREAKING AND ENTERING / BURGLARY

**Definition:** Unlawfully entering a building with intent to commit a crime inside.

**Variants:**
- Residential Burglary
- Commercial Burglary
- Auto Burglary
- Home Invasion (occupied dwelling)

**Key Questions to Ask:**
- Was anyone home?
- How did you get in?
- What did you take?
- Was this planned or opportunistic?
- Have you done this before?
- Did you case the place first?
- Where's the stuff now?

**Legal Consequences (US General):**
| Type | Prison Time |
|------|-------------|
| Commercial (unoccupied) | 1-5 years |
| Residential (unoccupied) | 2-10 years |
| Occupied dwelling | 5-20 years |
| With weapon | 10-25 years |

---

### DRUG DISTRIBUTION

**Definition:** Manufacturing, transporting, or selling controlled substances.

**Variants:**
- Street-level dealing
- Mid-level distribution
- Trafficking (large quantities)
- Manufacturing/Production
- Prescription fraud

**Key Questions to Ask:**
- What substance?
- How much are we talking about?
- Who do you sell to?
- Who supplies you?
- How long have you been doing this?
- Is this about money or addiction?

**Legal Consequences (Federal Guidelines):**
| Substance/Amount | Minimum | Maximum |
|------------------|---------|---------|
| Marijuana (100+ plants) | 5 years | 40 years |
| Cocaine (500g+) | 5 years | 40 years |
| Heroin (100g+) | 5 years | 40 years |
| Fentanyl (40g+) | 5 years | 40 years |
| Meth (50g+) | 10 years | Life |

**Additional Consequences:**
- Asset forfeiture (cars, cash, property)
- Loss of federal benefits (student loans, housing)
- Immigration consequences
- Enhanced penalties in school zones
- RICO charges if gang-affiliated

## TIER 2: SERIOUS MISDEMEANORS (Medium)

### THEFT / LARCENY

**Definition:** Taking someone else's property without permission or intent to return it.

**Variants:**
- Shoplifting
- Package theft ("porch piracy")
- Workplace theft
- Pickpocketing
- Bicycle/vehicle parts theft

**Key Questions to Ask:**
- What did you take?
- Why that item specifically?
- Is this the first time?
- What did you do with it?
- Do you understand who this affects?

**Legal Consequences:**
| Value | Classification | Consequences |
|-------|---------------|--------------|
| Under $500 | Petty Theft | Fine, community service |
| $500-$1,000 | Misdemeanor | Up to 1 year jail |
| Over $1,000 | Felony | 1-5 years prison |

---

### VANDALISM / PROPERTY DESTRUCTION

**Definition:** Willfully destroying or damaging property belonging to another.

**Variants:**
- Graffiti
- Vehicle damage (keying, slashing tires)
- Window breaking
- Arson (overlaps with felony)

**Key Questions to Ask:**
- Whose property was it?
- Why did you target this person/place?
- How much damage did you cause?
- Is this about revenge?
- Do you understand restitution?

**Legal Consequences:**
| Damage Amount | Consequences |
|---------------|--------------|
| Under $400 | Misdemeanor, fine, restitution |
| $400-$10,000 | Up to 1 year, restitution |
| Over $10,000 | Felony, 1-3 years, full restitution |

---

### DUI / DWI (No Injuries)

**Definition:** Operating a vehicle while impaired by alcohol or drugs.

**Key Questions to Ask:**
- How much did you have to drink?
- Where were you coming from?
- Did you think you were okay to drive?
- Has this happened before?
- Do you have a problem with alcohol?

**Legal Consequences:**
| Offense | Consequences |
|---------|--------------|
| First DUI | License suspension, fine, DUI school |
| Second DUI | Jail time (days-months), interlock device |
| Third DUI | Felony, prison time, long-term revocation |

---

### HARASSMENT / STALKING

**Definition:** Pattern of conduct that causes fear or emotional distress.

**Variants:**
- Cyber harassment
- Physical stalking
- Workplace harassment
- Revenge activities

**Key Questions to Ask:**
- How long has this been going on?
- Why this person?
- Do you understand how this affects them?
- What were you trying to accomplish?
- Will you stop?

**Legal Consequences:**
| Severity | Consequences |
|----------|--------------|
| First offense | Restraining order, misdemeanor |
| Violation of order | Jail time (months) |
| Aggravated stalking | Felony, 1-5 years |

## TIER 3: MORAL/PERSONAL CRIMES (Light)

These are not typically criminal but carry significant moral weight.

### GHOSTING (Relationship Abandonment)

**Definition:** Disappearing from someone's life without explanation.

**Relevant Questions:**
- How long were you together?
- What made you choose silence over conversation?
- Do you know what happened to them after?
- Have you done this before?
- What would you say if you had to face them now?

**"Consequences" (Social/Moral):**
- Damage to reputation
- Pattern that affects future relationships
- Guilt that surfaces later
- Becoming what you feared

---

### BETRAYING A FRIEND'S TRUST

**Definition:** Breaking confidence, lying, or acting against a friend's interests.

**Variants:**
- Revealing secrets
- Lying about them
- Stealing from a friend
- Getting involved with their partner
- Abandoning them in crisis

**Relevant Questions:**
- Why did they trust you?
- What did you gain from this?
- Do they know what you did?
- How would you feel if roles were reversed?
- Can this be repaired?

---

### INFIDELITY

**Definition:** Breaking a commitment of romantic exclusivity.

**Relevant Questions:**
- How long did this go on?
- Does your partner know?
- Why didn't you end the relationship first?
- What were you looking for?
- What do you plan to do now?

---

### LYING TO FAMILY ABOUT SOMETHING SIGNIFICANT

**Variants:**
- Hidden debt
- Secret addiction
- Hidden relationship/child
- Career/education lies
- Health issues concealed

**Relevant Questions:**
- How long has this lie been going on?
- Who else knows the truth?
- What are you afraid of?
- What happens when they find out?
- Is the lie protecting them or protecting you?
