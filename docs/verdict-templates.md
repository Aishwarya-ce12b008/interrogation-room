# Verdict & Resolution Templates

> *"The interrogation ends. But consequences are just beginning."*

## END STATE TYPES

### 1. FULL CONFESSION — COOPERATIVE

**Trigger:** Suspect admits to crime fully with genuine accountability.

**Template:**
```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                    INTERROGATION CONCLUDED
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CASE STATUS: CONFESSION OBTAINED

Suspect has provided a full confession to [CRIME TYPE].

COOPERATION LEVEL: High
REMORSE ASSESSMENT: [Genuine / Partial / Minimal]
VICTIM IMPACT: [Description]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                      RECOMMENDATION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Based on your full cooperation and acknowledgment of wrongdoing,
I will recommend the following to the District Attorney:

CHARGES: [Specific charges]
RECOMMENDED SENTENCE: [Sentence range]
MITIGATING FACTORS NOTED:
  • [Factor 1]
  • [Factor 2]
  • [Factor 3]

ADDITIONAL REQUIREMENTS:
  • [Restitution / Community service / Treatment / etc.]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                    DETECTIVE'S FINAL STATEMENT
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

"[Personalized closing from detective]"

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

### 2. FULL CONFESSION — UNCOOPERATIVE PATH

**Trigger:** Suspect confessed but only after significant resistance.

**Template:**
```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                    INTERROGATION CONCLUDED
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CASE STATUS: CONFESSION OBTAINED (AFTER RESISTANCE)

Suspect has confessed to [CRIME TYPE] following extended 
interrogation.

COOPERATION LEVEL: Low — confession obtained under pressure
REMORSE ASSESSMENT: [Assessment]
INITIAL DECEPTION: Yes — suspect provided false/misleading 
                   statements before confession

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                      RECOMMENDATION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Your eventual confession is noted. However, your initial 
attempts to deceive this investigation will be included in 
our report.

CHARGES: [Specific charges]
RECOMMENDED SENTENCE: [Sentence range — higher end]

AGGRAVATING FACTORS:
  • Attempted deception
  • [Other factors]

The District Attorney will make the final determination.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                    DETECTIVE'S FINAL STATEMENT
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

"We got there eventually. Would've been easier for everyone 
if you'd been straight from the start. Think about that next 
time you're tempted to lie."

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

### 3. LAWYER UP

**Trigger:** Suspect explicitly requests legal counsel.

**Trigger Phrases:**
- "I want a lawyer"
- "I want an attorney"
- "I'm not saying anything without a lawyer"
- "I plead the fifth"
- "I invoke my right to counsel"
- "I want to remain silent"
- "This interrogation is over"
- "I'm done talking"

**Template:**
```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                    INTERROGATION SUSPENDED
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The suspect has invoked their right to legal counsel.

Per Miranda v. Arizona (1966), all questioning must cease 
immediately upon invocation of the right to an attorney.

CASE STATUS: PENDING
CONFESSION: NOT OBTAINED
NEXT STEPS: Case proceeds without suspect cooperation

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                    DETECTIVE'S FINAL STATEMENT
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

"That's your right. You're entitled to it. 

But understand this — the investigation doesn't stop because 
you stopped talking. We're going to keep looking. We're going 
to keep asking questions. And when we find what we're looking 
for, you won't have a chance to explain your side.

Think about that while you're waiting for your attorney."

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Note: Interview terminated at [TIMESTAMP]
Suspect remanded to custody pending arraignment.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

### 4. EMOTIONAL BREAKDOWN

**Trigger:** Suspect shows signs of severe emotional distress.

**Template:**
```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                    INTERROGATION PAUSED
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Suspect is experiencing emotional distress.

DETECTIVE ACTION: Offering brief pause

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

*Detective slides a glass of water across the table*

"Take a minute. I know this is hard. 

Look — what you're feeling right now? That's normal. It means 
you understand the weight of what happened. That's actually 
important.

But we're not done here. When you're ready, we need to finish 
this conversation. The truth doesn't go away just because it's 
painful to say."

*waits*

"Okay. Let's continue. Tell me what happened."

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

### 5. SUSPECT WALKS / REFUSES TO ENGAGE

**Trigger:** Suspect consistently refuses to participate meaningfully.

**Template:**
```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                    INTERROGATION CONCLUDED
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CASE STATUS: INCONCLUSIVE — SUSPECT NON-COOPERATIVE

Suspect has declined to engage meaningfully with this 
investigation.

CONFESSION: NOT OBTAINED
COOPERATION LEVEL: None

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                      NEXT STEPS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Investigation will proceed through:
  • Physical evidence analysis
  • Witness interviews
  • Documentation review
  • [Other investigation methods]

Charges may be filed based on evidence obtained independently 
of suspect's statement.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                    DETECTIVE'S FINAL STATEMENT
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

"You had a chance to tell your side. You chose not to take it.

That's fine. We don't need your confession to make this case. 
We've built cases on less. And when those charges come down, 
remember this moment. Remember that you could have explained 
yourself, and you chose silence.

We're done here. For now."

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

## VERDICT VARIATIONS BY CRIME TIER

### TIER 1 (FELONIES) — Verdict Language

**Cooperative:**
```
"You're facing serious charges. But you did the right thing 
today. You told the truth. That matters to judges, to parole 
boards, to the people who'll decide how much of your life 
this takes. 

Get a good lawyer. Pay back what you can. And don't make me 
see you in this room again."
```

**Uncooperative:**
```
"We have what we need. You made this harder than it had to be, 
and that's going to cost you. The DA doesn't look kindly on 
people who waste the state's time.

You'll be charged with [CHARGES]. Get your affairs in order."
```

---

### TIER 2 (MEDIUM) — Verdict Language

**Cooperative:**
```
"You made a mistake. A real one. But you owned it, and that 
counts for something. 

Here's what's going to happen: [CONSEQUENCES]. It's not nothing. 
But it's a lot less than it could have been. Don't waste your 
second chance."
```

**Uncooperative:**
```
"You came in here, admitted to [CRIME], and then spent an hour 
trying to convince me it wasn't a big deal. It is a big deal. 
And your attitude just made it worse.

[CONSEQUENCES]. Deal with it."
```

---

### TIER 3 (MORAL/PERSONAL) — Verdict Language

**Cooperative:**
```
"This isn't something I can charge you with. There's no law 
against [moral failing]. 

But you know what you did. And now you have to live with it. 
The question is: what are you going to do about it? Are you 
going to make it right? Or are you going to carry this around 
until it poisons everything else in your life?

That's not a question I can answer for you. But I think you 
already know."
```

**Uncooperative:**
```
"You walked in here and told me what you did. And then you 
spent the whole time explaining why it doesn't matter. Why 
it wasn't your fault. Why the other person deserved it.

I've heard a lot of rationalizations in this room. Yours 
aren't special.

I can't make you feel guilty. I can't make you apologize. 
But one day, this is going to catch up with you. When you're 
alone, when it's quiet, when you can't distract yourself 
anymore — you're going to remember this conversation. And 
you're going to wish you'd been honest. Not with me. With 
yourself."
```

## SPECIAL RESOLUTIONS

### Confession With Accomplices

```
"You told us about [ACCOMPLICES]. That's going to help you. 
A lot. The DA values cooperation, especially when it leads 
to bigger fish.

Here's how this works: you give us everything — names, dates, 
how it worked. In exchange, I'll push for a deal. Reduced 
charges, maybe immunity on some of it. But you have to give 
us everything. Hold anything back, and the deal's off.

We understand each other?"
```

### Confession Revealing Larger Crime

```
"Wait. Hold on. What you just described — that's not [ORIGINAL 
CRIME]. That's [MORE SERIOUS CRIME].

*pauses*

Okay. We need to talk about this very carefully. What you're 
telling me changes things. I need you to understand the weight 
of what you're admitting to."
```

### Confession That Implicates Others

```
"So you're saying [OTHER PERSON] was involved. That's a serious 
accusation. I need to know: is this true, or are you trying to 
spread the blame around?

Because if you're lying about someone else's involvement, 
that's a whole new charge. Think carefully before you answer."
```
